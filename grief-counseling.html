Grief Counseling in Boulder & Denver, CO

Compassionate, skilled support for grief and loss of every kind. Individual grief therapy and facilitated support groups in Boulder and via telehealth.

Grief Deserves Real Attention

Our culture is often uncomfortable with grief. We rush through it, minimize it, or try to "fix" it before it has run its natural course. The result is often grief that goes underground — unprocessed, unnamed, and quietly undermining your wellbeing for years.

I approach grief differently. I believe grief is a natural and healthy response to love and connection. It doesn't need to be rushed or resolved — it needs to be witnessed, expressed, and integrated. That's what grief counseling is for.

What Grief Counseling Addresses

Grief isn't limited to the death of a loved one. Many losses deserve the same care and attention:

  • Death of a spouse, partner, parent, child, sibling, or friend
  • Complicated grief or prolonged grief disorder
  • Anticipatory grief (preparing for an expected loss)
  • Pet loss
  • Divorce and the end of long-term relationships
  • Loss of a pregnancy or infertility grief
  • Loss of identity (career, role, health, relationship status)
  • Empty nest grief
  • Grief after estrangement from a family member
  • Grief accumulation — when multiple losses compound over time

My Approach to Grief Work

I draw on several specialized grief-informed modalities tailored to where you are in your process:

  • Grief Therapy (narrative and meaning-making approaches) — helping you find a way to carry loss without being crushed by it
  • EMDR — particularly effective for traumatic loss or grief stuck in the body
  • Somatic approaches — working with the physical sensations of grief that don't respond to talk alone
  • IFS — understanding the parts of you that are grieving alongside the parts trying to protect you from it
  • Positive psychology integration — not bypassing grief, but ultimately supporting post-traumatic growth

Grief Support Groups in Boulder

In addition to individual grief therapy, I facilitate small, intimate grief support groups that offer something individual work can't: the profound experience of being truly understood by others who are also grieving.
