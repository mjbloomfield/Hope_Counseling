Therapy · Women in midlife

Women's Therapy in Boulder & Denver, CO

For women in midlife. Change, loss, possibility. Career, relationships, sexuality, hormones, caring for elders, identity, and the question of what comes next.

The Chapter That Asks for an Honest Reckoning

Midlife asks a lot of women all at once: aging parents, kids who are launching or already launched, careers that may need reinvention, partnerships that may need re-negotiating, a body that's changing, and underneath all of it the quiet but persistent question of who you actually are now that the structures of younger life have shifted.

Therapy is for the parts of that reckoning that need to be processed, healed, or understood at a root level, in a space that's honest and at a pace that belongs to you.

What Women Bring to Therapy

Career questioning, job loss, retirement

The work of reckoning with what you've built and what it cost, what's still alive inside it, and what wants to come next. This is as much identity work as it is practical planning.

Relationship distress, loss of passion, disconnection

For long-term partnerships that have lost their thread, where the communication patterns have hardened and the work is to either find each other again or get clarity about a different path forward.

Parenting, launching children, empty-nesting

The shift from being needed in one way to being needed in another (or barely needed at all), with the mourning, freedom, and identity questions that come along with figuring out what to do with the time and energy that comes back.

Divorce, breakups, "dating while gray"

For the end of a marriage and everything it asks of you, including re-entering dating after years or decades, and the slower work of rebuilding a sense of self that isn't defined by the partnership you were just inside.

Grief, loneliness, regret

For the losses that accumulate quietly through midlife, including friendships that have thinned and decisions you wish you'd made differently, with the work being to grieve them honestly rather than around them.

Health challenges, hormonal change, loss of vitality

For the perimenopause and menopause years, when energy and sleep and libido and mood are all shifting, and the psychological dimension of those physical changes asks for attention that medicine often can't quite name.

Sexual dissatisfaction and dysfunction

An area that's both underdiscussed and undertreated, where therapy can be a useful place to talk about it without the shame or weirdness that often surrounds it elsewhere.

Caring for elders, financial and family concerns

For the sandwich-generation reality of decisions about aging parents, alongside the financial and emotional weight of being the person everyone else leans on.

Anxiety, fear of death, identity crises

For the existential weight that midlife carries, including the question of who you are now and the related question of who you actually want to become next.

Questions about purpose and legacy

For the larger questions about what this chapter has been for, what you want the next one to be about, and what you want to leave behind when you're done.

Therapy or coaching, which fits? Therapy is the right container when something needs to be worked through, healed, or understood at a root level, and coaching is the right one when you already have a clear direction and want structured support to get there. Plenty of women do some of each.
