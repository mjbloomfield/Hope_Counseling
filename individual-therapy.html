Individual Therapy in Boulder & Denver, CO

Private, tailored therapy for adults navigating anxiety, grief, life transitions, burnout, identity questions, and more. In-person in Boulder and telehealth statewide.

A Space That Is Entirely Yours

Individual therapy offers something rare: dedicated time and space to focus entirely on yourself — your patterns, your pain, your hopes, and your next steps. No one else's needs in the room. No managing others' reactions. Just you, your therapist, and honest work.

I work with adults across a range of issues, with a particular specialty in the concerns that tend to cluster in midlife (ages 35–65). Whether you're in acute distress or simply at a crossroads, individual therapy can provide the clarity, skills, and support to move forward.

What I Help With

😰

Anxiety

Persistent worry, overthinking, perfectionism, and anxiety about health, relationships, work, or the future. Both general anxiety and more specific anxious patterns.

🍂

Grief & Loss

Loss of a person, a relationship, an identity, or a life chapter. Grief therapy that honors the full complexity of your experience.

🔄

Life Transitions

Major changes — career shifts, divorce, relocation, retirement, health diagnoses — that require more than willpower to navigate.

🔥

Burnout

Deep exhaustion — emotional, physical, and vocational. Restoring a sense of self beyond your role and responsibilities.

🪞

Identity & Self-Esteem

Questions about who you are, who you want to be, and whether you're living in alignment with your values. Self-worth that doesn't depend on performance.

💔

Relationship Patterns

Recurring relational difficulties, codependency, attachment issues, or difficulty with intimacy — addressed in individual work before or alongside couples therapy.

My Therapeutic Approach

I don't apply a single formula to every client. Instead, I draw on a range of evidence-based modalities and tailor the approach to what you're navigating:

  • EMDR (Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing) — for trauma, stuck grief, and anxiety rooted in past experiences
  • Internal Family Systems (IFS) — understanding the "parts" of yourself that may be in conflict
  • Somatic Therapy — working with the body's stored stress and trauma responses
  • Positive Psychology — building on existing strengths, not just reducing deficits
  • Psychodynamic Exploration — understanding how early experiences shape present patterns
  • Solutions-Focused Techniques — practical, goal-oriented strategies when appropriate
  • Mindfulness-Based Approaches — present-moment awareness to interrupt anxious or depressive spirals
