Your Therapist

Hi — I'm Hope.

D. Hope Tola, MA, NCC, LPCC · Boulder, CO

I'm a Licensed Professional Counselor Candidate with a Master's in Clinical Mental Health Counseling from the Family Institute at Northwestern University and a certificate in Positive Psychology from Harvard Medical School.

My work is built on a simple belief: the people who come to me deserve directness, not hedging — and genuine care, not clinical distance. I specialize in couples, adults in midlife (35–65), and parent–teen relationships.

I use the Gottman Method, Relational Life Therapy (RLT), IFS, EMDR, somatic approaches, and positive psychology — always tailored to you, never off a checklist.

My Approach

Evidence-Based Methods, Tailored to You

I don't apply a single formula to every client. Each person — each couple — gets a carefully considered mix of modalities based on their goals, history, and strengths.

  • Gottman Method — research-backed tools for couples to rebuild connection and manage conflict
  • Relational Life Therapy (RLT) — direct, relational approach to couples work
  • Internal Family Systems (IFS) — understanding the parts of yourself holding pain
  • EMDR — trauma processing through bilateral stimulation
  • Somatic & Mindfulness — body-based awareness alongside talk therapy
  • Positive Psychology — building on strengths, not just fixing problems
  • Solutions-Focused — practical, forward-moving strategies
  • Grief Therapy — specialized support for loss and bereavement

Getting Started

Starting Is Easier Than You Think

The first step is often the hardest. Here's what to expect.

1

Reach Out for a Free 15-Min Call

Tell me a little about what's going on, ask any questions, and see if working together feels right. No pressure, no commitment.

2

Complete a Short Intake Form

Sent securely before your first session so we can focus our time on what matters.

3

Come In (or Log On)

In person at the Boulder office or via HIPAA-compliant video. We'll explore your goals and begin building a path forward.

4

Build Momentum at Your Pace

Therapy is a process. We adjust as you grow. Most clients notice meaningful shifts within the first several sessions.
