Branch · Therapy

Therapy in Boulder, Denver & All of Colorado

Honest, evidence-based therapy for couples, family relationships, and adults navigating midlife. In person at the Boulder office and via HIPAA-compliant telehealth statewide.

"Therapy is for the moments when something needs to be understood rather than just managed, when a pattern has to shift at the root, when you've already tried thinking your way through it and you're still stuck."

Therapy Specialties

Where Therapy Lives

Pick a specialty to read more about it, or schedule a free 15-minute call and we'll work out together which approach fits where you are.

Couples Therapy

Evidence-based work using the Gottman Method, Relational Life Therapy, and EFT, for everything from premarital prep through long partnerships in distress.

Adult Children & Parents

Family work for grown adults and their (often aging) parents, where old roles still get in the way of the adult-to-adult relationship you both actually want.

Teen & Parent

Structured family sessions designed for the parent and teen as a pair, rather than treating the teenager as the problem to be fixed.

Parents of Young Kids

Family therapy that works with the parent (or both parents) on the family system itself, looking at patterns, alignment, and the climate the kids are growing up inside.

Men's Therapy

For men in midlife navigating divorce, career change, identity questions, or any of the other things that men often struggle to find a real space to talk about.

Women's Therapy

For women in midlife working through the various changes the chapter brings, from career and relationships to hormones, caring for elders, and the question of what's next.

01

Couples Therapy

For couples whose communication has started going in circles, where the same arguments keep repeating, or where there's been a real breach of trust. I integrate the most evidence-supported couples frameworks to fit your particular relationship.

Couples therapy →
02

Adult Children & Parents

For grown adults working through old family-of-origin dynamics with parents who are still very much in their lives, often as those parents are aging. The aim is the adult-to-adult relationship that hasn't quite been able to settle.

Adult-child & parent work →
03

Teen & Parent

Designed for one parent and one teen (13+) to work on the relationship between them, rather than the all-too-common arrangement where a teenager is sent to therapy alone to be "fixed."

Teen & parent →
04

Parents of Young Kids

Family therapy with the parent or parents, leaving the young kids at home, because the family system is shaped almost entirely by the adults inside it and that's where useful change tends to come from.

Parents of young kids →
05

Men's Therapy

I especially like working with men, particularly those navigating midlife divorce, a career reckoning, intimacy issues, or a deeper question of who they're becoming. The room is straightforward and not afraid of the conversation.

Men's therapy →
06

Women's Therapy

For women working through the changes midlife tends to bring, including career questions, the way long partnerships shift, hormonal change, and the larger question of identity in a chapter that frequently asks for one.

Women's therapy →

Getting Started

What the First Step Looks Like

1

Free 15-minute call

You tell me a little about what's going on and ask whatever you want to ask, with no pressure or commitment to book anything afterward.

2

Brief intake

A short form sent securely so we can use the first session well.

3

First session

You come in person to the Boulder office or join over HIPAA-secure video, and we name what you're actually working on and begin from there.

4

Build at your pace

Therapy unfolds over time, though most clients notice meaningful shifts within the first several sessions.
