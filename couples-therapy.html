Couples Therapy in Boulder & Denver, CO

Evidence-based couples counseling for communication, conflict, trust, and reconnection — using the Gottman Method, RLT, and EFT. In person in Boulder and telehealth statewide.

When Couples Therapy Can Help

Every relationship hits rough patches. But there's a difference between normal friction and patterns that erode your connection over time. Couples therapy isn't just for relationships in crisis — it's for any partnership where both people want things to be better.

Common reasons couples seek therapy:

  • Communication that goes in circles and never resolves
  • Feeling like roommates rather than partners
  • Repeated arguments about the same issues
  • A breach of trust — including infidelity or emotional affairs
  • Disconnection, loneliness, or loss of intimacy
  • Parenting disagreements putting strain on the relationship
  • Navigating a major life transition together (retirement, empty nest, career change, illness)
  • Premarital preparation for a strong foundation
  • Wanting to strengthen an already good relationship

My Approach to Couples Work

I draw on three of the most evidence-supported frameworks in couples therapy, integrating them based on what your relationship needs:

Gottman Method Couples Therapy

Developed from over 40 years of research by Drs. John and Julie Gottman, this method gives couples concrete tools to understand patterns of conflict, build friendship and admiration, and navigate difficult conversations without the "Four Horsemen" (criticism, contempt, defensiveness, and stonewalling) destroying the interaction.

Relational Life Therapy (RLT)

Created by Terry Real, RLT is a direct, no-nonsense approach to relational work. It focuses on patterns inherited from family of origin, the impact of patriarchy on relationships, and building genuine mutual intimacy. RLT doesn't hedge — and clients who appreciate directness tend to find it highly effective.

Emotionally Focused Therapy (EFT)

EFT addresses the deeper emotional attachment patterns driving conflict. When you and your partner argue, you're often not really arguing about the dishes — you're arguing about feeling unseen, unimportant, or unsafe. EFT helps couples identify and restructure these patterns at their root.

Both partners don't need to be "on board" to start. Sometimes one partner is more uncertain about therapy. That's okay. I work with wherever each person is. The first session can be exploratory — no commitment required beyond showing up.

What Couples Sessions Look Like

Sessions are 50–60 minutes and typically held weekly, especially in the early stages of treatment. My style is active and engaged — I won't just sit and watch you argue. I'll intervene, redirect, and coach in real time when that serves the work.

Couples often describe sessions as productive but sometimes uncomfortable in useful ways — the kind of discomfort that leads to real shifts, not just temporary relief.
