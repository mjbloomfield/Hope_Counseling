Midlife Therapy in Boulder & Denver, CO

Specialized counseling for adults ages 35–65 navigating the unique and complex terrain of midlife — identity shifts, transitions, relationships, purpose, and more.

Midlife Is Underserved. It Doesn't Have to Be.

We talk a lot about adolescence as a time of identity formation, and rightfully so. But midlife — roughly the years between 35 and 65 — is a second, equally profound identity reckoning that gets far less attention. This is the time when many people quietly ask: Is this really my life? Who am I now that the kids are older? What comes next?

These questions aren't signs of a crisis or failure. They're signs of a life being lived — and of a person paying attention. But they deserve more than a dismissive "that's just what happens at 40." They deserve honest, skilled engagement.

Midlife therapy is my primary specialty. I've trained specifically to work with the particular challenges adults face in the 35–65 window, and I bring both professional expertise and genuine enthusiasm for this stage of life to every session.

What Midlife Therapy Addresses

Midlife brings a unique convergence of stressors that rarely appear in isolation. Common themes I work with include:

🧭

Identity & Purpose

Who are you now that your role has changed — as parent, partner, professional? What do you want the next chapter to look like?

🪺

Empty Nest

The transition to an empty nest brings a complex mix of pride, loss, relief, and disorientation. Therapy helps you process all of it.

💑

Relationship & Marriage Changes

Midlife often surfaces long-standing relationship issues. Couples therapy, divorce support, and re-coupling guidance all available.

💼

Career Transitions

Career pivots, burnout, retirement planning, and the question of meaningful work after decades in one field.

👴

Aging & Health

Navigating your own health changes, chronic illness, and the role of caretaker for aging parents — often simultaneously.

🔍

Anxiety & Existential Questions

Midlife is fertile ground for existential anxiety about mortality, legacy, and whether it's "too late" for the life you imagined.

💔

Divorce & Re-Coupling

Divorce after 20+ years, navigating co-parenting with adult children, and starting over romantically in your 40s or 50s.

🌱

Grief & Loss

Midlife often brings a accumulation of losses — parents, relationships, health, former selves. Specialized grief support available.

My Approach to Midlife Work

I take a solutions-focused, positive psychology-informed approach to midlife therapy that doesn't ignore the genuine difficulties of this stage but also refuses to pathologize normal human development. The goal isn't just to cope — it's to build a life you actually want in the second half.

I draw on IFS (Internal Family Systems) to understand the competing "parts" of yourself that may be in conflict during a midlife transition, combined with psychodynamic exploration of how early patterns are showing up in present relationships and choices. I also use somatic and mindfulness techniques for clients who find that talk alone doesn't fully reach what needs to shift.

My Harvard Positive Psychology training directly shapes how I approach midlife work: not as a crisis to survive, but as an opportunity for intentional reinvention.

You don't have to be in crisis to benefit from midlife therapy. Many clients come not because something is catastrophically wrong, but because they want support navigating a significant transition thoughtfully. That's exactly the right reason to reach out.
