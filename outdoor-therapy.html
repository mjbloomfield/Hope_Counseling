Nature-Based Therapy

Outdoor & Walk-and-Talk Therapy in Boulder, CO

For clients who find that movement and nature open something that sitting across a desk can't. Therapy sessions conducted outdoors in Boulder's trails and green spaces.

Therapy Doesn't Have to Happen on a Couch

Walk-and-talk therapy is exactly what it sounds like: a therapy session conducted while walking, outdoors. It's not a new idea — Freud famously walked with some of his patients — but its evidence base has grown considerably as nature-based therapeutic approaches have gained recognition.

Boulder, Colorado, is an ideal setting for this work. We are surrounded by open space, trails, and natural beauty that can itself be part of the therapeutic experience.

Who Benefits from Outdoor Therapy

Walk-and-talk therapy isn't for everyone — but for certain clients, it's transformative:

  • People who feel physically restless or constrained in traditional office settings
  • Those who find eye contact during difficult topics overwhelming — side-by-side conversation can feel safer
  • Athletes and active individuals who relate to the world through movement
  • Clients working through grief, anxiety, or transitions who find nature regulating
  • Anyone who has noticed they think more clearly while moving
  • Clients doing coaching work where active engagement and forward motion feel appropriate

Note: Outdoor sessions are available to established individual clients after an initial in-office or telehealth session. This allows us to establish a therapeutic frame before moving the work outside.

What Research Says About Nature & Therapy

A growing body of research supports the benefits of nature-based interventions for mental health:

  • Time in nature reduces cortisol (stress hormone) levels
  • Walking has well-documented benefits for depression and anxiety
  • Side-by-side interaction can reduce social threat and facilitate more open communication
  • Nature exposure supports attention restoration, benefiting focus and reflection
  • Physical movement can facilitate emotional processing, particularly for trauma held in the body

How Outdoor Sessions Work

1

Start with an Initial In-Office or Telehealth Session

We establish our therapeutic relationship and agree on goals before moving outside.

2

Choose a Location Together

We select a walking route that works for your mobility, the content of our work, and privacy considerations.

3

Conduct the Session Like Any Other

The content and depth of outdoor sessions is the same as any therapy session. The setting changes; the quality of the work doesn't.

4

Adjust as Needed

On days when indoor work is more appropriate — emotionally or due to weather — we can always shift to the office or telehealth.

Frequently Asked Questions

Research suggests it can be equally effective for many presenting concerns — and more effective for some, particularly anxiety, depression, and trauma work where somatic engagement is valuable. The key is using it as a tool when it genuinely serves the client, not as a novelty.

We choose routes with privacy in mind, and we're mindful in public settings. Clients often find they self-regulate what they share naturally in outdoor environments — which is worth discussing openly before choosing outdoor sessions.

We have flexibility to move sessions indoors or to telehealth when weather makes outdoor work impractical. Colorado weather is unpredictable, and we plan for that.
