Therapy · Men in midlife (women welcome too)

Men's Therapy in Boulder & Denver, CO

Real, direct, useful work — for men navigating midlife divorce, career change, identity, sexuality, grief, purpose. (And yes — I work with women too. This page just makes clear that I especially like working with men.)

I Especially Like Working With Men

Men are often poorly served by therapy that doesn't quite know what to do with them — that hedges, that defaults to softness when directness would land better, that makes the room feel like it's for someone else. I've found I do my best work with men who are skeptical of therapy and need it to be something other than what they expected.

That doesn't mean it's "harder" or "softer" or anything in particular — it means it's actual engagement. A real conversation. Honest. Direct. Useful.

What Men Bring to Therapy with Me

Career questioning, job loss, retirement

The work that defined the first half is no longer working — and the question of what's next is bigger than just the next job. Reckoning with identity, ambition, money, and what you actually want.

Divorce, breakups, "dating while gray"

The end of a marriage. The aftermath. Re-entry into dating after years or decades. Rebuilding a sense of self that isn't defined by the role you were just in.

Relationship distress, loss of passion, disconnection

Inside a marriage or partnership that's still going but feels distant. Communication patterns that have hardened. Intimacy that has thinned. Real work on what's between you and your partner.

Sexual dissatisfaction and dysfunction

Underdiscussed and undertreated. Therapy can be a place to talk about it — without shame, without assumptions, without it being weird.

Grief, loneliness, regret

The losses that accumulate. The friendships that thinned out. The decisions you wish you'd made differently. Real grief work, not bypass.

Health challenges, hormonal change, loss of vitality

The body doing things it didn't used to do. Energy, libido, sleep, mood. The psychological dimension of physical change.

Caring for elders, financial strain

Middle-life squeeze: aging parents, growing or launching kids, work pressure. The reality of being the one everyone else is leaning on.

Anxiety, fear of death, identity crises

Existential weight that doesn't have an easy outlet. The question of who you are now that the structures of younger life have softened or fallen.

Questions about purpose and legacy

What was this for? What do I want to leave behind? What do I want the next chapter to actually be about?

Therapy or coaching — which fits? Therapy is right when something needs to be worked through, healed, or understood at a root level. Coaching is right when you have a clear direction and need structured support to get there. Many men do some of each. We can decide together in a free consultation.
