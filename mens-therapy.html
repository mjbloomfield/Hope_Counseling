Therapy · Men in midlife (women welcome too)

Men's Therapy in Boulder & Denver, CO

Real, direct, useful work. For men navigating midlife divorce, career change, identity, sexuality, grief, purpose. (And yes. I work with women too. This page just makes clear that I especially like working with men.)

I Especially Like Working With Men

Men are often poorly served by therapy that doesn't quite know what to do with them — therapy that hedges, that defaults to softness when directness would land better, or that simply makes the room feel like it's for someone else. My best work tends to be with men who are skeptical of the whole enterprise and need it to be something other than what they were expecting.

That doesn't mean the work is harder or softer or anything in particular; it just means it's a real conversation, where I'm engaged with you rather than performing a process at you.

What Men Bring to Therapy with Me

Career questioning, job loss, retirement

The work that defined the first half of life often stops working, and the question of what comes next tends to be bigger than just the next job. There's identity, ambition, money, and the slower question of what you actually want, all in the same conversation.

Divorce, breakups, "dating while gray"

For the end of a long marriage and everything that follows, including re-entering dating after years or decades, and the work of rebuilding a sense of yourself that isn't defined by the role you've just stepped out of.

Relationship distress, loss of passion, disconnection

For the inside of a marriage or partnership that's still technically going but has gone quietly distant, where the communication patterns have hardened and intimacy has thinned, and where what's needed is real work on what's actually between you and your partner.

Sexual dissatisfaction and dysfunction

Both underdiscussed and undertreated, this is something therapy can be a useful place to talk about, without the shame or weirdness that often surrounds it elsewhere.

Grief, loneliness, regret

For the losses that accumulate quietly through midlife, including the friendships that have thinned and the decisions you wish you'd made differently, where the work is to grieve them honestly rather than around them.

Health challenges, hormonal change, loss of vitality

For when the body starts doing things it didn't used to do, with energy and libido and sleep all shifting, and the psychological dimension of physical change asking for some attention of its own.

Caring for elders, financial strain

For the middle-life squeeze of aging parents, kids who are still growing or just launching, and work pressure all at once, with the underlying reality that you're often the one everyone else is leaning on.

Anxiety, fear of death, identity crises

For the existential weight that doesn't have an easy outlet, particularly the question of who you are now that the structures of younger life have softened or fallen away.

Questions about purpose and legacy

For the larger questions about what all of this has been for, what you want to leave behind, and what you want the next chapter to actually be about.

Therapy or coaching, which fits? Therapy is the right container when something needs to be worked through, healed, or understood at a root level, and coaching is the right one when you already have a clear direction and want structured support to get there. Plenty of men do some of each, and we can decide together in a free consultation.
