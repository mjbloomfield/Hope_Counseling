Insurance & Payment

Do You Take Insurance?

Hope Counseling & Family Therapy is an out-of-network provider. This means I do not bill insurance directly, but many clients are able to receive partial reimbursement through their out-of-network benefits.

How to use your out-of-network benefits: After each session, I can provide you with a "superbill" — a detailed receipt with all the billing codes your insurance requires. You submit this to your insurer and they reimburse you directly at your out-of-network rate. Many PPO plans reimburse 50–80% of session fees after your deductible is met. Call your insurance company and ask about your "out-of-network mental health benefits" to understand what you'd be reimbursed.

Accepted Payment Methods

  • All major credit and debit cards
  • HSA and FSA cards (therapy is a qualified HSA/FSA expense)
  • Check or cash

Open Path Collective

Hope Counseling participates in the Open Path Psychotherapy Collective, which provides reduced-rate therapy to clients experiencing financial hardship. If cost is a barrier, please mention this during your consultation and we can discuss what options are available.

Cancellation Policy

Please provide at least 24 hours' notice for cancellations. Late cancellations and no-shows are charged the full session fee, as that time cannot be filled for another client on short notice.

The Real Cost of Therapy

Therapy is an investment in your mental health, your relationships, and your quality of life. Consider what poor mental health, a struggling relationship, or a stalled transition costs you — in time, energy, wellbeing, and even financial decisions made from a difficult mental state. Viewed this way, effective therapy often pays dividends well beyond its direct cost.

Still have questions about cost? Bring them to your free 15-minute consultation. There's no pressure, and discussing finances openly is part of finding the right fit.
