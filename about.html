Therapy & Coaching

Hello — I'm Hope.

D. Hope Tola, M.A., N.C.C., L.P.C.C. · Hope Counseling & Family Therapy, PLLC

I'm a therapist based in Boulder, Colorado, and a coach who works with clients anywhere in the world. My specialties are couples, adults navigating midlife, and family relationships — adult children with their parents, teens with parents, and parents of younger kids working on the family system itself.

I believe the people who come to me deserve honest engagement — not platitudes, not a one-size-fits-all formula, but real, skilled attention to where they are and what they're navigating.

What I Do, and Why

I work with two kinds of clients: people who need therapy to heal, process, or change something at the root, and people who want coaching — forward-focused work toward a clear goal or transition.

The work is built on a simple belief: you deserve honest, direct, and caring engagement. Not platitudes. Not a one-size-fits-all advice column. Real, skilled attention to where you are — and a path forward you can actually take.

Therapy (Colorado)

My therapy practice — Hope Counseling & Family Therapy, PLLC — serves individuals, couples, and families in Boulder and throughout the Denver metro area, in person and via HIPAA-compliant telehealth statewide. As a Licensed Professional Counselor Candidate (L.P.C.C.) in Colorado, I work clinically with the full range of life challenges people bring to therapy — relationship distress, midlife shifts, grief, identity questions, family conflict, anxiety, and the slow accumulating weight of things that haven't been said.

Coaching (Worldwide)

Coaching is a different kind of work, and it's not bound by state license. I coach clients anywhere in the world — most often men and women in midlife who are navigating a major transition and want structured, psychologically-grounded support to move through it well. Career questioning, relationship reckoning, parenting and launching, the question of what comes next — these are coaching territory.

You can read more about my approach to coaching specifically on the coaching page — and about the larger midlife framework I bring to it, on midlifewell.com.

Education & Training

My foundation comes from rigorous graduate training and ongoing professional development:

  • Master of Arts in Clinical Mental Health Counseling — The Family Institute at Northwestern University, one of the country's premier family-therapy training programs
  • Certificate in Positive Psychology — Harvard Medical School
  • National Certified Counselor (N.C.C.) — National Board for Certified Counselors
  • Licensed Professional Counselor Candidate (L.P.C.C.) — State of Colorado

Specialized Clinical & Coaching Training

  • Gottman Method Couples Therapy — research-based approach for rebuilding connection and managing conflict
  • Relational Life Therapy (RLT) — direct, relational approach developed by Terry Real
  • Internal Family Systems (IFS) — understanding and healing the parts of yourself that hold pain
  • EMDR — trauma reprocessing via bilateral stimulation
  • Somatic & Mindfulness Techniques — body-aware approaches to complement talk work
  • Positive Psychology — building on strengths, not just fixing problems

Who I Especially Like Working With

I have a particular love for working with men in midlife — a population that is often poorly served by therapy that doesn't quite know what to do with them. I find this work meaningful and effective, and I'd encourage any man considering whether therapy or coaching is "for him" to reach out for a no-pressure conversation. I also work extensively with women in midlife, with couples at every stage, and with family relationships — the parent–teen pair, adult children with their aging parents, and parents of younger kids working on the family itself rather than the kids.

My Approach

Honest, Direct, Caring, and Solutions-Focused

I describe my style as honest, direct, caring, and solutions-focused. I bring warmth and humor to the work while also challenging clients to grow. I draw on a range of psychodynamic, somatic, and positive-psychology interventions — always tailored to the individual or couple in front of me.

I use cards, games, and apps when they serve the work. I'm never dogmatically wedded to a single framework. And I'm comfortable saying so when something isn't fine — and equally comfortable saying so when it is.

I've lived through my own midlife transitions. I understand the terrain is both challenging and full of possibility — and I bring that lived sense to every session.
