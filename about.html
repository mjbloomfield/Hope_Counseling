What I Do, and Why

I work with two kinds of clients. The first come in for therapy, when something needs to be healed, understood, or changed at the root. The second come in for coaching, when there's a clear goal or transition ahead and the work is more about getting from here to there.

My basic belief is that the people I work with deserve genuine attention to where they actually are, and a workable path forward from that point. That's a different thing from advice or reassurance.

Therapy (Colorado)

My therapy practice, Hope Counseling & Family Therapy, PLLC, serves individuals, couples, and families in Boulder and throughout the Denver metro area, both in person and via HIPAA-compliant telehealth statewide. As a Licensed Professional Counselor Candidate in Colorado, I work clinically with whatever people bring through the door, from relationship distress and midlife shifts to grief, identity questions, family conflict, anxiety, and the slow accumulating weight of things that haven't been said out loud.

Coaching (Worldwide)

Coaching is a different kind of work, and it's not bound by state license, so I'm able to coach clients anywhere in the world. Most often I'm working with men and women in midlife who are navigating a major transition and want psychologically-grounded support to move through it well. That tends to mean career questioning, relationship redesign, parenting transitions, and the larger question of what they want the next chapter to be about.

You can read more about my approach on the coaching page, and about the larger midlife framework I bring to it at midlifewell.com.

Who I Especially Like Working With

I have a particular love for working with men in midlife, who are often poorly served by therapy that doesn't quite know what to do with them. The work tends to be both meaningful and effective, and any man wondering whether therapy or coaching is for him is welcome to reach out for a no-pressure conversation. I also do a lot of work with women in midlife, with couples at every stage of partnership, and with family relationships in their various configurations: parents and teens, adult children and aging parents, and parents of younger kids who want to work on the family itself rather than on the children.

My Approach

Warm and Direct, with Real Skin in the Game

My style is candid and warm at the same time. I bring humor into sessions and I'll challenge you when challenging you is what serves the work, drawing on the Gottman Method, Relational Life Therapy, positive psychology, and the various play, grief, and family-systems trainings I've pursued over the years. I'll use cards, games, or apps when they help, and I'm not dogmatically attached to any single framework.

I'm comfortable saying when something isn't actually okay, and equally comfortable saying when it is. I've lived through my own midlife transitions, so I understand that the terrain can be both genuinely difficult and full of possibility, and I bring that lived sense into the room.
