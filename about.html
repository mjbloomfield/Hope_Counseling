More About My Background & Approach

I'm a Licensed Professional Counselor Candidate (LPCC) and National Certified Counselor (NCC) based in Boulder, Colorado. My private practice, Hope Counseling & Family Therapy, PLLC, serves individuals, couples, and families in Boulder and throughout the Denver metro area — in person and via HIPAA-compliant telehealth.

My work is built on a simple belief: you deserve honest, direct, and caring support — not platitudes or one-size-fits-all advice, but real engagement with where you are and where you want to go. Whether you're in the middle of a relationship crisis, a midlife identity shift, or a grief that feels impossible to carry, I'm here to walk alongside you with both professional skill and genuine compassion.

Education & Training

My foundation in therapy comes from rigorous graduate training and ongoing professional development:

  • Master of Arts in Clinical Mental Health Counseling — The Family Institute at Northwestern University, one of the country's premier family therapy training programs
  • Certificate in Positive Psychology — Harvard Medical School
  • National Certified Counselor (NCC) — National Board for Certified Counselors
  • Licensed Professional Counselor Candidate (LPCC) — State of Colorado

Specialized Clinical Training

In addition to my graduate degree, I've pursued advanced training in the evidence-based modalities I use most with couples and families:

  • Gottman Method Couples Therapy — research-based approach for rebuilding connection and managing conflict
  • Relational Life Therapy (RLT) — direct, relational approach to couples work developed by Terry Real
  • Internal Family Systems (IFS) — understanding and healing the parts of yourself that hold pain
  • EMDR — trauma reprocessing via bilateral stimulation
  • Somatic & Mindfulness Techniques — body-aware approaches to complement talk therapy
  • Grief Therapy — specialized support for bereavement and complicated grief

Who I Work With

I have a particular love for working with adults in midlife (roughly ages 35–65) — a stage of life that is often underserved despite being one of the most complex. Midlife brings simultaneous demands: aging parents, launching children, career reckonings, shifting identities, and relationship stress. I find this work deeply meaningful.

I also work extensively with couples — at every stage, from those who want to strengthen an already good relationship to those on the edge of separation. My approach with couples is honest and direct: I won't tell you everything is fine if it isn't, but I will work hard with you to find a path forward.

My third focus area is parent–teen family work — supporting the critical relationship between parents and adolescents as it evolves, sometimes painfully, through the teenage years.

My Approach

I describe my style as honest, direct, caring, and solutions-focused. I bring warmth and humor to the work while also challenging clients to grow. I draw on a range of psychodynamic, somatic, and positive psychology interventions — always tailored to the individual or couple in front of me. I use cards, games, and apps when they serve the work, and I'm never dogmatically wedded to a single framework.

For some clients, I offer walk-and-talk outdoor therapy sessions — conducted in Boulder's beautiful natural surroundings for those who find movement and nature conducive to deeper conversation.

Outside the Office

I've lived through my own midlife transitions and understand that the terrain is both challenging and full of possibility. I bring personal experience, intellectual curiosity, and a genuine belief in people's capacity for change to every session.
