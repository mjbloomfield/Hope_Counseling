Coaching: When You Know What You Want to Do

Coaching and therapy serve different — though often complementary — purposes. Therapy is for healing, understanding, and processing. Coaching is for those with a clear goal or challenge who want structured, forward-focused support to move through it effectively.

My coaching work is informed by my clinical training — which means you get more than cheerleading. You get someone who understands the psychological dimensions of whatever you're working on, and who can help you build a realistic, sustainable path toward your goals.

Coaching sessions are often conducted outside the traditional office setting — at a coffee shop, on a walk, at a location meaningful to the work you're doing. This flexibility is intentional.

Achievement Coaching

Achievement coaching supports individuals with targeted goals — whether professional, personal, creative, or athletic. Common coaching clients include:

  • Adults navigating a major career transition or professional reinvention
  • Creatives working through blocks, perfectionism, or creative identity
  • Athletes working on mental performance alongside physical training
  • Entrepreneurs or leaders addressing clarity, focus, and decision-making
  • Midlife adults wanting support with "what's next" planning and execution
  • Anyone with a goal that feels stuck despite motivation and effort

Parent Coaching

Parent coaching is for parents who want strategic support in navigating a challenge with their child or teen — without necessarily bringing the child into sessions (though family therapy is also available).

Parent coaching is particularly valuable for:

  • Parents of teenagers struggling with communication or conflict
  • Parents navigating a child's mental health challenges and needing guidance on how to respond
  • Parents feeling lost in the shift from parenting young children to parenting adolescents
  • Co-parents needing to align on approach despite personal conflict
  • Empty-nesting parents recalibrating their relationship with an adult child

Note: Coaching is distinct from therapy. It is not covered by insurance and does not address mental health diagnoses. If your needs overlap with therapy, we can discuss the most appropriate path in a consultation.
