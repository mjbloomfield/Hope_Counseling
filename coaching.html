What I'm Doing & Why

Coaching for the Midlife Reckoning

Midlife is rarely a smooth on-ramp into the next chapter. The structures that organized the first half of life (the job, the marriage, the parenting role, the body itself, and the assumptions about what matters) tend to soften or shift in ways that ask for a real response.

Coaching is for the part of that reckoning that wants forward motion. Where therapy might dig into what something means about your childhood, coaching is more interested in the practical question: what do you actually want next, and how do you get there? That can mean a career change, the redesign of a long relationship, the question of purpose, or the larger work of becoming a version of yourself that hasn't existed yet.

Because I bring clinical training to coaching, you get more than cheerleading. You get someone who understands the psychological terrain of whatever you're moving through and can help you build a sustainable path toward what you actually want, rather than a generic plan.

Much of this work draws on the framework I write about at midlifewell.com, my second site, focused entirely on the midlife transition.

How Coaching Can Help

What People Bring to Midlife Coaching

The themes that show up in midlife therapy also show up in coaching, just from a different angle. Coaching is forward-focused: less about excavating where something came from, more about what you want to build and how to actually move toward it.

💼

The work of figuring out what's still alive in your career and what wants to come next, holding the practical planning and the identity questions in the same conversation.

💞

Relationship Distress & Disconnection

For long partnerships that have lost some of their thread, this is forward-facing work on what you want the next chapter of the relationship to actually be.

Couples Coaching

Forward Work on the Partnership

Couples coaching sits in different territory than couples therapy. Where therapy works on what's broken or unhealed in a relationship, coaching is about what wants to be designed or built next, looking at alignment, intention, and the shape of the next chapter of the partnership.

Common starting points include empty-nest couples redesigning the partnership, couples navigating retirement together, long-term couples who want to deepen rather than simply maintain, couples planning a significant life change (a move, a sabbatical, a business), and premarital couples who want structured forward planning rather than clinical premarital therapy.

If what you actually need is healing or repair after distress, infidelity, or chronic conflict, the right container for that is couples therapy.

Career & Transition Coaching

The "What's Next" Question, Made Workable

For midlife adults whose relationship to work is shifting, whether through career change, professional reinvention, retirement, or the slow question of whether what you're doing is still what you want to be doing.

This kind of coaching blends practical career strategy with the deeper identity work midlife tends to require. You can't really "pick a new job" if the larger question is who you're becoming, so we work both layers at once.

  • Adults in mid-career wondering whether to keep going or pivot
  • People preparing for or recently entering retirement who want to figure out what comes next
  • Job loss, and the work of re-entering professional life on your own terms
  • Creative work where blocks, perfectionism, or identity questions are getting in the way
  • Entrepreneurs and leaders working on clarity, focus, and decision-making
  • "What's next" planning when there's no obvious answer to land on

Coaching or Therapy?

Which Fits Where You Are

The shortest answer: therapy is for what needs to heal, and coaching is for what wants to be built. Plenty of clients do some of each, sequentially or in parallel.

Coaching is for you when…

  • You have a clear direction and need structured support to move
  • You're not in crisis but you're stuck
  • You want forward focus, not historical work
  • You want a thinking partner, not a clinician
  • You live outside Colorado (coaching is worldwide; therapy is Colorado-only)

Therapy is for you when…

  • Something needs to be healed, not just navigated
  • Old patterns from family of origin are still alive
  • There's grief, trauma, anxiety, or relational distress to work through
  • You need a clinical container with a licensed therapist
  • You're in Colorado and qualify for in-state therapy
