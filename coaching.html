What I'm Doing & Why

Coaching for the Midlife Reckoning

Midlife is rarely a smooth on-ramp into the next chapter. It's a reckoning. The structures that organized the first half — the job, the marriage, the parenting role, the body, the assumptions about what mattered — start to soften, shift, or fall.

Coaching is for the part of that reckoning that wants forward motion. Not "what does this mean about my childhood" — that's therapy. What do I want next, and how do I actually get there? Career change, relationship redesign, the question of purpose, the work of becoming someone who hasn't existed yet.

I bring my clinical training to coaching — which means you get more than cheerleading. You get someone who understands the psychological dimensions of whatever you're working on, and who can help you build a realistic, sustainable path toward what you actually want.

Much of this work draws on the framework I write about at midlifewell.com — my second site, focused entirely on the midlife transition.

Couples Coaching

Forward Work on the Partnership

Couples coaching is different from couples therapy. Therapy works on what's broken or unhealed. Coaching works on what's possible — design, alignment, the next chapter of the relationship.

Common starting points:

  • Empty-nest couples redesigning the partnership
  • Couples navigating retirement together
  • Long-term couples wanting to deepen rather than just maintain
  • Couples planning a major life change together — move, sabbatical, business
  • Premarital couples wanting structured forward planning (vs. clinical premarital therapy)

If what you need is healing or repair after distress, infidelity, or chronic conflict, look at couples therapy — that's the right container for that work.

Career & Transition Coaching

The "What's Next" Question, Made Workable

For midlife adults reckoning with work — career change, professional reinvention, retirement, the question of whether what you're doing is still what you want to be doing.

This work blends practical career strategy with the deeper identity work midlife requires. You can't just "pick a new job" if the bigger question is who you're becoming. We work both at once.

Coaching or Therapy?

Which Fits Where You Are

The shortest answer: therapy is for what needs to heal. Coaching is for what wants to be built. Many clients do some of each.

Coaching is for you when…

  • You have a clear direction and need structured support to move
  • You're not in crisis but you're stuck
  • You want forward focus, not historical work
  • You want a thinking partner, not a clinician
  • You live outside Colorado (coaching is worldwide; therapy is Colorado-only)

Therapy is for you when…

  • Something needs to be healed, not just navigated
  • Old patterns from family of origin are still alive
  • There's grief, trauma, anxiety, or relational distress to work through
  • You need a clinical container with a licensed therapist
  • You're in Colorado and qualify for in-state therapy
