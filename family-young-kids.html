Family Therapy for Parents of Young Kids

The work happens with the parent (or both parents), without the young kids in the room. The aim is the family system itself, not the children.

Working on the Family Without Working on the Kids

I don't work directly with kids under 13, but the family of a young child is still a system, and that system is shaped almost entirely by the adults inside it. When parents do their own work (together or one parent alone), the climate the kids grow up in shifts in noticeable ways.

This page is for parents who know something needs to change in the family and are willing to look at their own part of it, with the kids staying home while the adults do the work.

When This Is the Right Container

  • Co-parenting is harder than parenting because you and your partner aren't aligned
  • One of you is reacting to the kids in ways that mirror your own family of origin, and you'd like that to stop
  • The household climate has gotten tense, sharp, or distant in ways that are getting harder to ignore
  • You're a single parent navigating it solo and want a thinking partner
  • A divorce or separation is in process and you want the family system to land well on the other side
  • Something specific has happened (a loss, an illness, a move) and the family is wobbling under it
  • You want to parent more like the parent you actually want to be

How the Work Goes

Sessions can include one or both parents, weekly or every other week. We work on the patterns you bring into parenting, the dynamic between co-parents, and the practical stuff, always with the underlying system in view.

If what looks like a parenting question is really a partnership question, couples therapy may turn out to be the better entry point, and we can decide together in a free consultation.

Note on age: If your child is 13 or older, look at teen & parent therapy, where the teen is included as a full participant in the work.
