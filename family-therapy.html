Family Therapy in Boulder & Denver, CO

Strengthening family relationships — especially parent-teen pairs — through better communication, understanding, and connection. In-person in Boulder and telehealth statewide.

Family Relationships Take Real Work

Families are among our most important — and most complicated — relationships. The closeness that makes family bonds so meaningful also means that conflict, miscommunication, and old patterns can feel impossibly stuck.

My family therapy work focuses particularly on parent–teen and parent–young adult relationships: the critical bond between parents and adolescents or young adults as it evolves through some of the most turbulent developmental years. I also work with adult family members navigating significant transitions together.

Parent–Teen Therapy: My Specialty

The parent–teen relationship is one of the most important in both parties' lives — and one of the most easily strained. Adolescents are doing exactly what they're supposed to do: individuating, testing boundaries, and forming their own identity. Parents are doing what they're supposed to do: maintaining connection and safety while letting go. The problem is that both can feel threatening to the other.

Family sessions at Hope Counseling are structured for the pair — parent and teen — not just the teen as the "identified patient." Because in my experience, what's needed is usually a shift in the dynamic between parent and child, not just a fix to the teenager.

Common Issues I Work With in Parent–Teen Therapy

  • Communication breakdowns and escalating conflict
  • Disconnection or emotional distance
  • Teen mental health concerns affecting the family system
  • Parenting disagreements between co-parents
  • Navigating boundaries, autonomy, and trust
  • Supporting LGBTQ+ teens and their parents
  • Family adjustment to college departure or return
  • Blended family challenges and step-parent dynamics
  • Divorce and co-parenting through the teen years

My Approach to Family Work

I approach family work with a systems lens — meaning I look at how each member of the family contributes to and is shaped by the patterns of the whole. I don't believe in labeling any family member as the "problem." Instead, I work to shift the patterns that keep everyone stuck.

With teens especially, I work to build a genuine alliance — not just as a proxy parent. Teens can tell when they're being managed, and they disengage. I bring honesty and respect to those relationships, which tends to make teens willing participants rather than reluctant ones.

Parent coaching also available. If your teen isn't ready for family sessions, parent coaching can help you shift your own approach in ways that create space for connection. See our coaching services page for details.
